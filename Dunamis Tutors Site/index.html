What is IJMB in Nigeria?

The IJMB programme is an advanced level (A' Level) educational scheme that enables students to gain direct entry admission into Nigerian universities without JAMB. Established by Ahmadu Bello University (ABU), Zaria, IJMB is widely accepted by many tertiary institutions across the country. With this program, students can secure 200-level admission into their preferred universities after successfully completing the IJMB examination.

How IJMB Works

The IJMB program runs for 9 to 12 months, during which students undergo intensive lectures in three subject combinations relevant to their desired course of study. Upon completion, students take the IJMB examination, and their scores determine their eligibility for direct entry into partner universities.

Which Universities Accept IJMB in Nigeria?

Many prestigious universities in Nigeria accept IJMB as a valid entry qualification. Some of these universities include Ahmadu Bello University (ABU), University of Ilorin (UNILORIN), University of Lagos (UNILAG), Obafemi Awolowo University (OAU), and many more. Our counselors will guide you in choosing the right university based on your preferred course.

Can I Use IJMB to Study Medicine and Surgery?

Yes! Many universities accept IJMB for admission into medical-related courses, including Medicine and Surgery, Nursing, Pharmacy, Medical Laboratory Science, and others. However, certain institutions may have specific IJMB cut-off marks for competitive courses like Medicine.
